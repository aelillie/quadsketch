\begin{abstract}
This paper analyzes, experiments, and verifies the relatively new algorithm \qs{} proposed in \cite{wagner17} and makes a contribution by modifying the decompression step of \qs{}, inserting random bits instead of zeros. A baseline algorithm has been implemented, which verifies the results from \cite{wagner17} on \sift{} and \mnist{} datasets. Additional experiments have been performed on \gist{} and a synthetic dataset, \clust{}. Experiments compare the performance of \qs{} against the modified algorithm, named \qsr{}. The experiments shows a better average case performance of \qsr{} in regards to accuracy and distortion over \qs{}, in particular on \mnist{}. Finally a theoretical proof of \qsr{} is given, showing a better expected distance preservation than \qs{}. 
\end{abstract}
